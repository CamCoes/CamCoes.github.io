2017 Steamworks Robot: Tina

2017 was a fun year. The 2017 game contained 3 major robot functions, shooting fuel, delivering gears to your steamship, and climbing a rope at the end of the match. Since 2017 was my first year on the team I didn't get to do as much as I would have liked to do on this robot. During the build season I mainly worked on vision tracking, during our needs and wants meeting we had determined that the robot should have vision tracking to self align itself with the target before shooting. In the end our vision tracking system contained a Raspbery PI 3, 2 usb cameras, and 2 green leds, and custom python code. This system was able to track retroreflective tape located on the target. In addition to vision tracking I also worked on the robots autonomous routines. During the first 15 seconds of the game all robots will move autonomously, our robot was able to do nearly all of the available tasks during this time. During the offseason I explored fabrication on the team by improving our climber. Originally our climber ran on one cim motor, my idea was to switch it to two 775 pro motors to improve speed and prevent breaker trips.

2018 Power Up: Lamar

2018 was a great year. The 2018 game contained 2 major robot functions, manipulating power cubes, and climbing. Power Up was my second year on the team, I was able to do alot more things that my first year on the team. This year I mainily worked on motion profiling and robot control. Motion profiling was used during the 15 second autonomous period and was capable of positioning the robot to a final target while following a preprogrammed path within a 6" tolerence (this was then improved in 2019). On top of motion profiling I also worked on other robot control such as binding specific robot function to the driver and operators controllers.

2019 Destination: Deep Space: Timby

While 2019 was not the best year that the team had at competitions it was one of the best for learning. During 2019 we designed and built a very complex robot with many aspects. The robot was a great learning tool teaching us what parts of the design were good and what was bad. This years game had 3 different major robot functions, manipulate cargo, manipulate hatch panels, and to climb a 6", 13", or 19" step. This was the first year that I really stepped outside of the programming aspect of the robot, I helped pick electrical sensor, and design custom circuits for the robot's different functions. After picking those sensor or designing them, I cadded custom cases for every sensor that didn't have one. In addition to helping electrical and CAD I also helped fabricate multiple axles, and custom cnc elevator rollers. Although I was helping the other subteams make the robot I still had plenty of time to develop a new vision tracking system and to update our motion profiling system to be accurate within 2".

2020 Infinate Recharge: Mantis

The 2020 season had the potential to be one of our best years however it was abruptly cut short by the 2020 COVID-19 pandemic. Our robot was built from all of our learning experiences from our 2017 to 2019 seasons. During this year I spent most of my time programming our new swerve drive. This year despite loosing most of our programmers to graduation our robot had some of our most complex control, the robot was equiped with coordinate based motion profiling, vision tracking (based on 2017), and many accurate pid controllers. Ontop of programming most of the robots control I also cadded the shooter mechanism for the robot.
